SMARTLOCK

INDUSTRIAL DESIGN

To redesign the traditional door lock with an IoT perspective to enhance the user experience

1 month internship project

BACKGROUND
  • Problem

    Keys can be lost, stolen, forgotten. Your hands can be full or it can be too dark to see the keyhole. Although ubiquitous, keys are not the perfect solution to a locked door. The aim of the new lock was therefore to draw upon and incorporate technology to eliminate the need for keys, or at least relegate them to a backup or last resort.

  • Environment

    Before designing a new lock, existing locks and their mechanisms needed to be examined and understood. At least initially, it would be important that the lock could replace existing locks without the need to also replace the door; the limiting dimensions therefore needed to be known. Different types and sizes of doors were also researched to get a feel for the context of the product.

  • Ideation

    An iterative process, intertwined with research, quick sketches help to develop ideas and illuminate possible solutions and potential obstacles. Different form factors were studied as well as potential features and how they may be incorporated into a design.

  • Functionality

    Research into similar systems was conducted to work out the best method of operation for automating the opening and closing of a lock. Potential features included: thumb print reader, wifi, Bluetooth, voice activation, camera and keypad. The feasibility of incorporating these was researched with primary limiting factors of power consumption, size and cost.

The final design used Bluetooth Low Energy to allow connection to be made with a smart phone. Lock can be controlled using smart lock app, granting users keyless access and allowing monitoring of lock.

A real time clock was incorporated onto the PCB to allow the user to share time-controlled digital ‘keys’ with people, remotely granting people access.

4 AA batteries were used to power the lock. Research revealed that they provided the greatest battery life and optimal voltage supply in comparison to other easily available batteries. This was preferred to using rechargeable lithium-ion batteries.

Designed around standard door thicknesses’, deadbolts and bore holes allowed for easy replacement of existing lock. Adjustable deadbolt provides a greater flexibility.

Exterior - 60mm x 60mm x 25mm

Interior - 90mm x 90mm x 45mm

When connected to home wifi, the lock would be compatible with personal virtual assistants (Siri, Amazon Alexa, Google Assistant), allowing for easier control.

Built around a classic deadbolt, users are still able to open and close the lock using a traditional key

CONCEPT DESIGN

Exterior of lock features an LED illuminated keypad for access via pin code

EXTERIOR

A traditional deadbolt is used, allowing users to keep their existing bolt if they wish.

LOCK

A DC motor drives a gear transmission to operate the lock

AUTOMATION

4 AA batteries are easily accessed by removing the back panel

COMMERCIAL

A concept to be used in a commercial environment was also developed. This design would replace current keypad and swipe card locks found in hotels and offices globally.

RESIDENTIAL

Although the circular design was chosen as the preferred design initially, other forms were also looked at for residential scenarios. It was identified that consumers may prefer a smarter and traditional form as opposed to the more futuristic circular design. It was therefore valuable to demonstrate potential future diversification to the client.
