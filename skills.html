SKILLS

As an engineer and as a designer, working within a team is essential. I have always relished working together with people to reach a common goal and believe that when done effectively, teamwork can produce greater results than the sum of its parts.

Working as lead-engineer on the majority of my projects at AutoPacific Group involved constant communication with all elements of the supply chain: company directors, our internal manufacturing (both onsite and international), purchasing, marketing and warehousing as well as external suppliers and testing facilities.

At both Design Destiny and LIC Energy I was involved with client meetings, project bids, and communicatngion with staff in different countries and contractors.

I have over 10 years CAD experience from school through university, internships and work. I have continually looked to expand my knowledge of CAD software and its capabilities, giving me greater freedom to model different forms. I have used Solidworks from 2012 up to the 2020 version. I also have experience with AutoCAD.

Although presentation quality renders were not required for the majority of my degree, I understood the importance of realistic visual aids to demonstrate my ideas. Consequentially I taught myself to use Keyshot whilst at university through online tutorials, experimentation and practice. Having started with Keyshot 5, I have continued to build my proficiency up until Keyshot 8 and subsequently used my rendering skills whilst at AutoPacific Group.

Access to well-equipped workshops at school, university and AutoPacific Group has allowed me to realize many of my ideas whilst building a practical knowledge of manufacturing techniques. I am able to take my knowledge of these different processes into consideration when exploring different design routes.

Understanding different stages of the manufacturing process also allows for effective communication with people responsible for manufacture of a design.

Experience includes: power tools, lathing, welding, soldering, bending, milling, CNC laser cutting and sand casting.

During lockdown in Melbourne I started to teach myself some basic computer programming.

Starting with Python, I have since learnt elements of HTML, CSS & JavaScript.

I have since experimented by building basic GUI projects (calculator, expense tracker, alarm clock) and several websites (including this one). I am looking forward to building my knowledge of back-end languages and experimenting more with data analysis applications.
